Problem Definition The advent of ultra-long-context language models and
sophisticated memory agents has fundamentally altered the landscape of natural
language processing. Recent architectures, such as the MemAgent framework, have
demonstrated the capacity to ingest and process arbitrarily long
documents—spanning up to 3.5 million tokens—with performance degradation
consistently remaining below the five percent threshold. 1 These systems achieve
this unprecedented scale by systematically segmenting input streams and
maintaining a fixed-length, compressed memory buffer managed via continuous
overwrite strategies. 3 However, while the empirical accuracy of these models on
retrieval and generation tasks is highly documented, a critical mechanistic
question remains unresolved: does the specific contextual information written
into and retained by the agent's memory buffer genuinely and causally drive the
model's downstream predictions, or is it merely correlative noise that the model
ignores in favor of its pre-trained parametric priors? Initial investigations
leveraging the Editability-Faithfulness (EF) metric reveal a stark disconnect
between semantic retention and causal utilization. The EF metric, historically
evaluated using parametric modification techniques like Rank-One Model Editing
(ROME), tests whether perturbing a specific factual association within the
model's weights forces a corresponding flip in the model's rationale-based
output. 5 When applied to base architectures such as GPT2-XL on the Fact
Extraction and VERification (FEVER) benchmark, the EF score hovers at a
remarkably low 12.5 percent. 7 This low causal grounding metric indicates that
the vast majority of context retained by memory-augmented models does not exert
a causal influence on the final generation. Such massive context inefficiency
presents a unique opportunity for aggressive, causal-driven context pruning. If
the specific chunks of text that actively drive decision-making can be isolated,
the surrounding non-causal noise can be aggressively discarded without degrading
downstream accuracy. This document serves as the implementation-first master
blueprint for the MemFaith project. Executed over a highly compressed four-week
sprint extending from March 30 to April 20, 2026, this initiative transitions
the theoretical framework of Editability-Faithfulness into a highly scalable,
inference-time pipeline. 7 Because generating gold-standard EF labels via
parametric edits (like ROME) for every textual chunk creates an insurmountable
computational bottleneck 7 , MemFaith pivots entirely to a Leave-One-Chunk-Out
(LOCO) ablation strategy. The primary problem definition requires the system to
ingest a long-context query heavily padded with Wikipedia distractors,
deterministically segment the context into discrete chunks, and perform highly
parallelized ablation inference. 7 By systematically hiding one chunk at a time
from the model and measuring whether the generated answer diverges from the
full-context baseline, the system computes the Causal Chunk Score (CCS). The
success criteria for this engineering sprint are strictly defined. First, the
team must deliver a fully reproducible, high-throughput CCS computation pipeline
capable of running deterministic ablation loops across both single-hop (FEVER)
and multi-hop (HotpotQA) datasets. 7 Second, the system must yield empirical
degradation curves plotting the CCS against increasing segmentation depths ( )
to validate the hypothesis that causal influence dilutes as memory pressure
increases. 7 Finally, contingent upon the stability of the core pipeline, the
project must demonstrate a behavioral distillation proof-of-concept. This
extension requires training a lightweight sequence classifier on the binary
causal labels generated during the ablation phase, effectively creating a pre-
inference chunk scorer that can prune long contexts dynamically. 7 The ultimate
metric of success for this discriminator is its ability to maximize the token
reduction ratio while maintaining a 95 percent faithfulness retention rate
relative to the full-context baseline answer. System Architecture Blueprints To
process tens of thousands of LOCO ablations across massive datasets within a
constrained temporal and computational budget, the MemFaith system is
architected as a strictly decoupled, seven-module data pipeline. This modularity
ensures that upstream preprocessing bottlenecks do not stall GPU-bound inference
clusters, and that the rigorous mathematical definitions of the causal metrics
are preserved without contamination from sampling variance. The pipeline
initiates with the Data Adapter and Long-Context Constructor modules. These
components are responsible for transforming pristine, short-form benchmark
queries into chaotic, high-pressure memory tasks. The system parses inputs from
FEVER and HotpotQA, extracting the gold-standard evidence passages. 7 To
simulate the operational environment of an unbounded memory agent, the
constructor injects a heavily padded payload of distractor passages sourced from
unstructured Wikipedia dumps. 9 The insertion logic randomly interleaves the
gold evidence among the distractors to explicitly counteract the "lost in the
middle" positional bias frequently observed in contemporary Large Language

Preprocessing Strategy The experimental integrity of the Causal Chunk Score
relies entirely on the quality of the long-context construction. Feeding a model
a context comprised of random, disjointed noise fails to replicate the
operational pressures of a true memory agent. The data preprocessing strategy
must intelligently fuse high-signal gold evidence with distributionally similar
distractor passages to create an environment where identifying the causal chunk
is genuinely difficult. FEVER (Fact Extraction and VERification) serves as the
primary single-hop analysis vehicle. 7 FEVER provides a meticulously annotated
set of claims mapped to specific Wikipedia evidence sentences, coupled with a
constrained, clean label space (SUPPORTS, REFUTES, NOT ENOUGH INFO). 7 This
clean label space is mathematically crucial for the early stages of the
pipeline, as it allows the Causal Comparator to execute flawless, programmatic
string matching without the noise introduced by semantic ambiguity. 7 HotpotQA
provides the necessary complexity for testing Hypothesis 2, which posits that
multi-hop tasks will exhibit more distributed and fragile causal structures. 7
HotpotQA questions are designed so that the model must synthesize information
from at least two distinct Wikipedia documents. 18 In the context of LOCO
ablation, if the model requires both Chunk A and Chunk B to reason effectively,
removing either chunk should independently trigger an answer flip. To transform
these datasets into long-context evaluations, we adopt the distractor injection
methodologies established by the HELMET and RULER benchmarks. 9 The
preprocessing strategy executes the following sequence: 1. Gold Evidence
Extraction: Isolate the exact sentences or paragraphs explicitly annotated as
necessary for resolving the query. 18 2. Distractor Retrieval: Utilizing BM25 or
dense retrieval (e.g., Contriever), search a unified Wikipedia dump to extract
passages that are semantically similar to the query but lack the causal facts
required to answer it. The use of distributionally similar distractors, rather
than random text, prevents the LLM from relying on simple semantic clustering to
find the answer. 20 3. Context Assembly: Concatenate the gold evidence and the
distractors. The position of the gold evidence must be randomized uniformly
across the sequence. If the evidence is consistently placed at the beginning or
end of the prompt, the LLM's inherent "lost in the middle" bias will skew the
CCS metric, as chunks near the boundaries will register artificially high causal
influence simply due to their position. 11 4. Format Standardization: Wrap the
final assembled context in a rigid prompt template: You are a highly accurate
fact-verification assistant. Read the following context carefully. [Context]
{long_context} [Query] {claim/question}. Training Pipeline and Experiment Plan

Analysis: MemFaith Causal Faithfulness **Date:** April 1, 2026 ## Executive
Summary MemFaith is a highly focused, four-week graduate NLP engineering project
designed to measure whether long-context Large Language Models (LLMs) genuinely
rely on causal evidence. Moving beyond the semantic approximations of
"Retrieval-Augmented Generation" (RAG) paradigms, MemFaith implements **Leave-
One-Chunk-Out (LOCO) ablation** to isolate the causal necessity of individual
context chunks. The project establishes a novel evaluation metric—the **Causal
Chunk Score (CCS)**—and optionally culminates in a behaviorally distilled chunk-
scorer capable of aggressively pruning long contexts without causing downstream
performance degradation. ## Problem Formulation and Scope * **The Illusion of
Memory:** While models such as MemAgent can ingest up to 3.5M tokens, prior
parametric editing metrics (like Editability-Faithfulness via ROME) suggest that
the vast majority of retained context does not causally drive the final
output.[1, 7] * **Scope Pivot:** Because parametric weight editing is
computationally prohibitive at scale, MemFaith pivots entirely to inference-time
**chunk ablation**. It tests causal necessity by asking: *If chunk $i$ is
removed from the prompt, does the model's answer flip?* * **Core Metric:**
Causal Chunk Score is defined mathematically as $CCS(K) = (1/K) \sum_{i}
Pr[A_{-i} \neq A_{full}]$. ## Architecture Deep-Dive To ensure rigorous causal
measurement, the system architecture operates across a strict 7-stage pipeline :
1. **Data Adapter:** Normalizes the query and evidence schemas for FEVER
(single-hop) and HotpotQA (multi-hop). 2. **Long-Context Constructor:** Injects
distributionally similar Wikipedia distractors around the gold evidence to
simulate memory pressure. 3. **Deterministic Chunker:** Splits the context into
$K$ chunks, utilizing `spaCy` sentence-boundary detection to prevent arbitrary
semantic fracturing mid-word. 4. **Inference Engine:** A batched, highly cached
generation loop (enforcing `temperature=0.0`) that evaluates the full context
alongside all $K$ ablated permutations. 5. **Causal Comparator:** Evaluates
answer flips. Uses strict regex string matching for FEVER, and Token-F1 coupled
with an LLM-as-a-judge fallback for the free-form text of HotpotQA. 6.
**Evaluation Harness:** Aggregates CCS scores and plots degradation against
increasing values of $K$. 7. **Behavioral Distillation (Extension):** Trains a
lightweight classifier head on the binary causal labels to serve as an efficient
pre-inference pruner. ## Model/Method Landscape * **Primary Baseline:** GPT2-XL
(1.5B) serves as the architectural anchor, maintaining strict empirical
continuity with historical ROME/EF benchmarks. * **Modern Competitor:**
Qwen2.5-1.5B acts as a secondary baseline, ensuring the findings hold up against
modern, instruction-tuned models with massive native context windows. *
**Pruning Baselines:** Frameworks like LLMLingua and Selective Context serve as
ideological competitors to the causal-scorer extension, relying on syntactic
surprisal rather than empirical causal necessity. ## Dataset and Preprocessing
Analysis * **FEVER:** Selected for its rigidly controlled `` label space,
allowing for flawless programmatic measurement of causal flips. * **HotpotQA:**
Essential for testing multi-hop reasoning, assessing whether causal dependency
is distributed across multiple chunks. * **Distractors:** Sourced via BM25
retrieval from Wikipedia dumps and interleaved randomly to counteract the LLM
"lost in the middle" positional bias. ## Evaluation Framework * **CCS
Degradation Curve:** The primary analytical output, testing the hypothesis (H1)
that finer segmentation dilutes causal influence across the prompt. *
**Stratification:** The harness slices CCS data by label type (e.g., analyzing
if `REFUTES` claims require higher causal dependency) and absolute chunk
position. * **Pruning Efficiency:** The discriminator extension is evaluated by
plotting the Token Reduction Ratio against Full-Context Accuracy Retention. ##
Training/Deployment Strategy The core CCS pipeline requires no training, relying
entirely on inference-time ablation. The training strategy is isolated to the
**Extension Track**. * **Behavioral Distillation:** Treating the ablation loop
as a label-generator, the pipeline pairs `[Query][Chunk]` inputs with binary
causal labels to train a fast sequence classifier (e.g., a Qwen2.5-0.5B head)
using Focal Loss to account for extreme class imbalance. * **Deployment:** The
resulting scorer acts as an ONNX-exported pre-processing filter, discarding low-
value tokens at the edge before they hit the expensive LLM context window. ##
Schedule The project is constrained to a highly aggressive 4-week sprint (Mar 30
- Apr 20, 2026) : * **Week 1:** Pipeline scaffolding, long-context construction,
and end-to-end smoke testing. * **Week 2:** FEVER ablation runs at scale;
generation of initial CCS tables and curves. * **Week 3:** HotpotQA adaptation;
triggering the Distillation Extension if the core pipeline is stable. * **Week
4:** Metric aggregation, final plotting, report writing, and reproducibility
packaging. ## Risks and Failure Modes * **Metric Integrity Loss:** Sloppy answer
matching on HotpotQA generates false "flips," destroying CCS validity.
*Mitigation: Strict F1 scoring combined with an LLM-as-a-judge fallback.*. *
**Segmentation Artifacts:** Arbitrary token splitting cuts mid-sentence, causing
flips due to grammatical destruction rather than causal necessity. *Mitigation:
Strict sentence-boundary snapping.*. * **Compute Explosion:** Running $K=16$
across tens of thousands of queries is computationally prohibitive. *Mitigation:
Aggressive SQLite caching and representative sub-sampling for high $K$ runs.*.
## Strengths * **Actionability:** Significantly more scalable and buildable than
full parametric weight editing (ROME). * **Empirical Rigor:** Provides clear,
empirical evidence of causal necessity over mere semantic similarity,
differentiating it sharply from basic RAG implementations. * **Clear Metric:**
The CCS provides a highly readable, quantifiable metric for tracking memory
efficiency. ## Potential Gaps / Concerns * **Weaker Intervention:** Inference-
time ablation is theoretically "weaker" than surgical weight editing, requiring
careful narrative framing to satisfy academic rigor. * **Synergistic
Relevance:** Single-chunk ablation completely ignores synergistic
relevance—scenarios where chunks A and B are useless individually but critically
causal when combined. * **Scope Creep:** Over-focusing on the Discriminator
extension before the CCS baseline is fully proven threatens overall project
completion. # work_division.md **Title:** Team Execution Plan: MemFaith
